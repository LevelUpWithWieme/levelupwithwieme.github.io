Our Story

How It All Began

It all started the night before our friend Skylar's wedding.

Dakota had known Skylar for most of her life — they met playing soccer around 2004. Nick had known Skylar since sixth grade, when he switched to public school.

The night before the wedding, Dakota and her dad were visiting Skylar's mom, Rachel. The conversation went something like this:
“Do you have a boyfriend?” “No.”
“Do you have a girlfriend?” “No.”
“Do you want one?” “Maybe...?”

One phone call later, Skylar told Nick, “I’m setting you up with my friend Dakota tomorrow.” And just like that, fate stepped in.

Falling in Love

Their first interaction was born from forgetfulness. They had forgotten the broom for the ceremony, and Dakota had the house keys — attached to Nora’s leash. She handed Nick the doggy bag holder (keys included), and off he went, putting the pedal to the metal in the poor Cadillac to go retrieve it.

Later that evening, after the ceremony, dances, and speeches, Nick mustered up the courage to make his move. He brought Dakota over to his side of the head table, and the two talked the night away.

It was a match made in heaven (not that either of them really believes in heaven).

Not long after, they made it official — though only after many "I like like yous." They fell deeply and totally in love, quickly becoming inseparable. Eventually, Dakota showed up at Nick’s for the weekend… and never really left. Over time, she officially moved in. It was the best time.

Growing Together

They even adopted their third kitty, Stella Luna (formerly known as Balsam Fir, the fourth of the tree kitten litter that Nick's sister fostered and Nick's parents adopted), rounding out their little family of five (Salem, Spirit, and Stella).

Their love continued to grow, rooted in shared adventures, daily joy, and enduring support through tough times.

The Proposal

After everything they’d experienced together, they were ready for the next step.

During a week-long camping trip to Lake George, Nick had a secret — the engagement ring hidden under the center console of the car.

The proposal was everything Dakota had dreamed of: filled with joy, shock, swearing (she’ll never stop swearing like a sailor — sorry, not sorry), and pure happiness.

Now, they’re counting down the days to say “I do” and begin the next chapter of their happily ever after.
